## A Quick Taste of What's Inside

### The Fish You're Probably Missing

Most visitors to Japan stick to maguro (tuna), sake (salmon), and ebi (shrimp). That's like visiting France and only eating baguettes. Here are three fish you need to try:

**Kohada (Gizzard Shad)** — This is the true test of an Edomae sushi chef. A small, silvery fish that requires precise vinegar curing. When done right, it's tangy, complex, and utterly addictive. If a restaurant serves excellent kohada, everything else on their menu will be good too.

**Engawa (Flounder Fin Muscle)** — The thin strip of muscle along the edge of a flounder. Slightly chewy, buttery, with a delicate sweetness. Most tourists don't know it exists.

**Chutoro (Medium Fatty Tuna)** — Everyone wants otoro (the fattiest cut), but most Japanese regulars prefer chutoro. It has the clean tuna flavor with just enough richness.

### The Seasonal Secret

Right now in spring, these fish are at their peak:
- **Sayori** (halfbeak) — delicate, translucent, elegant
- **Shima-aji** (striped jack) — clean, refined, exceptional
- **Tai** (sea bream) — "sakura-dai" in spring, beautiful pink flesh
